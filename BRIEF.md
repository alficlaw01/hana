# Scout Brief — Hana (Agentic Dating App)

## Your role
You are Scout, a commercial intelligence agent. Produce a VC-pitch-quality commercial analysis deck for Jason Bennett, a PE analyst at Clayton, Dubilier & Rice, who is evaluating building Hana — an agentic dating app.

## The concept
Hana is a dating app where your AI agent does the matching for you. Instead of swiping on photos, your agent communicates with other users' agents, assesses deep compatibility, then presents you with a pre-vetted match and a suggested first date — already planned around your mutual interests.

The elevator pitch: "Why settle for a good match when agents can find your one in 8 billion?"

The name "Hana" means both "one" (Korean/Japanese) and "flower" (Japanese) — carrying the concept of finding your singular match while evoking growth and beauty.

**The core insight:** Dating apps today monetise the *searching*. Hana monetises the *finding*. The bottleneck isn't meeting people — it's the weeks of stilted texting to assess basic compatibility. Two agents can compress that into minutes. You only show up when there's a real reason to.

## Your deliverable
Produce a compelling VC pitch deck saved as `DECK.md` covering:

1. **The Problem** — why dating apps are broken. Swipe fatigue, time wasted, shallow matching, the exhaustion of modern dating. Make the reader feel it.

2. **The Insight** — the specific unlock: AI agents already know everything about you. Two agents talking can do in minutes what takes humans weeks. The information gathering phase of dating is eliminated.

3. **The Solution** — how Hana works. Agent onboarding, agent-to-agent communication, match presentation, date suggestion. Walk through the user journey.

4. **Market Opportunity**
   - Dating app market size globally and UK
   - Number of single adults, demographic breakdown
   - AI dating / matchmaking market emerging trends
   - Why 2026 is the right moment (AI agent adoption curve)

5. **Competitor Landscape**
   - Map all dating apps: Tinder, Hinge, Bumble, Thursday, Raya, etc.
   - Any AI-first dating apps emerging (there are a few — find them)
   - What none of them do that Hana does
   - The white space

6. **Go-To-Market**
   - Target demographic (who feels this pain most acutely?)
   - Launch city strategy (London first?)
   - Viral mechanics — what makes people share this?
   - Influencer / press angle
   - How to get the first 1,000 users

7. **Business Model**
   - Freemium structure
   - Premium subscription (what's the premium tier?)
   - Comparable ARPU to Hinge/Bumble
   - Revenue projections at scale

8. **The Viral Angle**
   - Why this spreads — "my agent found me a date" is a story people tell
   - TikTok / social content potential
   - Network effects: more agents = better matching = more users

9. **Risks & Mitigations**
   - Privacy concerns (what does the agent know about you?)
   - Cold start problem (need agents on both sides)
   - Big Tech risk (Hinge/Bumble copies the feature)
   - Trust and safety

10. **Why Now, Why Win**
    - The moment AI agents go mainstream in consumer apps
    - First mover advantage
    - The team (Jason's background, domain insight)

## Tone
Founder pitching Sequoia. Confident, narrative-driven, emotionally resonant. This should make someone say "I want in." Not a dry report — a compelling story with data behind it.
